During Parma's big antiques fairs, finding a place to stay in town gets hard and pricey. If you're a collector, an exhibitor or simply an enthusiast heading in for **Mercanteinfiera**, basing yourself in Piacenza is a comfortable, quiet alternative.

## What Mercanteinfiera is

**Mercanteinfiera** is the large international fair of antiques, 20th-century design and collectibles held at Fiere di Parma. The Autumn 2026 edition runs **from 3 to 11 October**: nine days of furniture, mid-century design, jewellery and curiosities from around the world. It draws visitors from across Europe, so accommodation in Parma sells out fast.

## Why stay in Piacenza

Piacenza is a short hop from Parma but away from the fair-day crush. At Roma284 you sleep in a **whole apartment in the historic centre** — quiet, with a **private patio** and a fully equipped kitchen: perfect for coming back in the evening after a day at the fair. Entry is independent via **smart-lock self check-in** from 3 pm, and pets are always welcome, free of charge.

On top of that, Piacenza is just **33 minutes from Milan** by Frecciarossa: if you extend your stay, the Lombard capital is one train away.

## Getting to the fair from Piacenza

Fiere di Parma is reachable **in under an hour**: by train to Parma and then a shuttle/bus to the exhibition centre, or by car along the A1 in about 45 minutes. Piacenza station is a **9-minute walk** from the apartment (900 m), so you set off comfortably first thing. You'll find connection and timetable details on the [Getting here](/en/getting-here/) page, and more ideas for fair days in the [Trade fairs](/en/trade-fairs/) section.

- **Dates:** 3–11 October 2026
- **Piacenza to Parma:** under an hour (train or A1)
- **From the station to Roma284:** 9-minute walk (900 m)
