Piacenza is a compact, authentic city of art, perfect for a weekend away from the crowds. In 48 hours you can experience the best of it on foot, with no need for a car. Here's a tried-and-tested itinerary.

## Day 1 — The historic heart

### Morning

Start from **Piazza Cavalli**, the city's living room, with its two bronze equestrian statues by Mochi and the Palazzo Gotico dominating the square. A few steps away you'll find the **Cathedral**, a splendid Romanesque church from the 12th century: step inside to admire its frescoed dome.

### Afternoon

Spend the afternoon at **Palazzo Farnese**, home to the Civic Museums, where you'll find Botticelli's famous *Madonna* and the charming Carriage Museum. Then stroll through the **pedestrian area** among shops and historic cafés.

## Day 2 — Flavours and surroundings

### Morning

Dedicate the morning to food: visit a local market and taste the three PDO cured meats — coppa, pancetta and salame piacentino. A glass of **Gutturnio**, the local red, is the perfect match.

### Afternoon

Take a relaxed walk along the **River Po** or explore the streets around the centre. Before leaving, treat yourself to a typical first course such as **pisarei e fasò** in one of the town's traditional trattorias.

## Getting around

Everything is within walking distance: from Roma284 you reach the main sights in a few minutes. Leave the car behind and enjoy the city at a slow pace — that's the real charm of Piacenza.
