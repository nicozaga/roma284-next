Milan is perfect for a day trip, and staying in Piacenza makes it all more relaxing: no big-city prices, no evening chaos. Set off in the morning, enjoy Milan, and return in the evening to a quiet town. Here's how to plan it.

## Morning: the heart of Milan

Take an early train from Piacenza: with the Frecciarossa you're at **Milano Centrale in 33 minutes**. From the station, metro line M3 takes you to **Duomo** in minutes. Spend the morning on the cathedral — it's worth climbing the **rooftop terraces** among the spires — and the **Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II**. A short walk away are **La Scala** theatre and the fashion quarter.

## Afternoon: art or relaxation

In the afternoon, choose based on your interests. For art, the **Sforza Castle** and the **Pinacoteca di Brera** are unmissable (book Leonardo's *Last Supper* well ahead — slots sell out fast). For a more relaxed mood, wander the **Navigli** canals or the **Brera** district, both great for an aperitivo too.

## Getting around the city

The ATM urban ticket covers metro, trams and buses. The network is extensive: the metro reaches every area in minutes. Skip the car: in Milan, traffic, the Area C congestion charge and pricey parking make train + metro the smartest choice.

## The return

Trains between Milan and Piacenza run frequently until late evening, with last services around midnight. So you can stay for dinner or a concert without worrying about driving back. Once in Piacenza, the apartment is minutes from the station: end the day with the calm that Milan, at those prices and that pace, simply can't offer.
